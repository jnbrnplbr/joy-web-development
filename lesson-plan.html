PROJECT PRESENTATION

A. Engaging Activity

The teacher will let the students play “pass-the-message”. (Mechanical drawing meaning)

Example: Mechanical Drawing are architectural or engineering plans created with hard tools. It is sometimes called “blue prints”

B. Discussion

The teacher will discuss the difference of Manual Drawing and CAD. The students are expected to recite using their books.

C. Wrap up/ Generalization Guided Question: “Give the advantages and disadvantages of using CAD over traditional”.

D. Assessment

●Book Activity1 p. 9-10 (WRITTEN)

● Activity2: Making house plan (WRITTEN)

A. Engaging Activity The teacher will let the students observe the CAD Interface. After which, the teacher will let the students label the parts without looking at their books.

B. Discussion The teacher will check and discuss the CAD Interface. The teacher will use the tarpapel CAD Interface that was answered by the students.

C. Wrap up/ Generalization Guided Question: “Demonstrate the steps on how to open CAD and describe the parts.”

D. Assessment

●Activity3: Group (Identifying the Parts of CAD)

●Activity4: Opening CAD

A. Engaging Activity

The teacher will flash 5 command tools in using CAD. With the different objects, the student will connect it with the tools the object was made.

B. Discussion

The teacher will demonstrate how the command will be used. After which, the students will do it by theirselves.

C. Wrap up/ Generalization

Guided Question: “Give the uses of each command and what are the main shapes/objects it can create”.

D. Assessment

●Activity1: Demonstrate tools and Commands (AUTHENTIC)

●Oral Participation (Graded Recitation)

A. Engaging Activity

The teacher will demonstrate how to use the ruler in CAD. After which, students with the use of laptops will also try it.

B. Discussion

The teacher will discuss the important notes in using coordinates and points. After which, the students will do the activity.

C. Wrap up/ Generalization Guided Question: “What is the main use of ruler in coordinating points?

D. Assessment

●Activity 2: Book Activity p. 31-32 (WRITTEN)

●Activity 3: Demonstrate the use of Ruler (AUTHENTIC)

A. Engaging Activity

The teacher will give students in group a set of lines and shapes. Each group will create something out of the given shapes and lines. First group to finish will be given additional points in their recitation.

B. Discussion

The teacher will discuss the different tools in making lines and shapes.

C. Wrap up/ Generalization

Guided Question: “If you will be given a chance to create your own tool, what it will be? Why?”

D. Assessment

●Activity 4: Exploring CAD using lines and shapes (AUTHENTIC)

A. Engaging Activity (Continuation)

The teacher will demonstrate what are the things they can make out of shapes and lines. Afterwhich, students will think by their own and create something out of the lines and shapes.

B. Discussion

The teacher will discuss the different tools in making lines and shapes.

C. Wrap up/ Generalization

Guided Question: “If you will be given a chance to create your own tool, what it will be? Why?”

D. Assessment

●Activity 5: Making something out of lines and shapes (AUTHENTIC)

E. Engaging Activity

The teacher will show the CAD Interface. After which, students will recall the uses of each tools. (Recitation)

F. Discussion

The teacher will discuss the important notes in using CAD. (Open and Close File; Saving File) (Drawing Tab; Undo & Redo; Line and Shapes)

G. Wrap up/ Generalization Guided Question: “Give the advantages and disadvantages of using CAD over traditional when it comes in making shapes”.
